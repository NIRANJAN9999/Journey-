PHOTO GUIDE
===========
Put your image files in this folder, then swap the matching <div class="media-placeholder" ...>
in index.html for <img src="assets/filename.jpg" alt="...">.

JOURNEY SECTION (aspect ratio 4:3, except the "Then / Now" pair below which is 1:1)
- A remote-control car — even a current photo of the same kind of toy car works if you
  don't have one from childhood. This just needs to be recognizable, not literal.
- The motor teardown or the copper-wire fix — a close-up shot works well here, it doesn't
  need to be the original motor.
- CAD work, machining, or your diploma workshop — a screenshot of a CAD file, or a photo
  from the workshop/lab, either is fine.
- Your Arduino / ESP32 breadboard setup — any photo of a board mid-project works.

FULL CIRCLE MOMENT (two square 1:1 photos, shown side by side)
- "Then" — a remote-control toy car like the ones you took apart as a kid.
- "Now" — the Bluetooth-controlled car you built at 20.
This pair is the one moment on the page designed to be memorable — it's worth taking a
bit more care with these two photos than the others. Similar framing/angle between the
two photos will make the "then vs now" read instantly.

WORK SECTION (aspect ratio 16:10, wide)
- The finished RC car.
- Your home automation setup — the ESP32 wiring, or a screenshot of the Google Home app
  controlling it.
- 3D-printed idols or resin pieces.
- The lamp, in progress — doesn't need to be finished, "in progress" is the point.
- The server or a terminal screen from the multi-agent system.

OG COVER IMAGE
- assets/og-cover.jpg — 1200x630px. This is what shows up as the preview image when
  someone shares your link on LinkedIn, WhatsApp, etc. A clean photo of one of your
  builds, or even a simple text-on-color card with your name, works well here.

General tips:
- Consistent lighting/background across photos makes the page feel curated rather than
  a random dump of images — doesn't need to be a studio, just consistent.
- If you don't have a photo for a given spot yet, it's fine to leave the placeholder box
  in place for now — it won't break anything, it'll just show the dashed outline and label.
